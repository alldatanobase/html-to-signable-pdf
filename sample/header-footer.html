Field Operations Manual

This manual describes standard operating procedures for field technicians. The header above and the page numbering below repeat on every page; they are written once in the source and lifted into each page's margins automatically.

1. Preparation

Before departing for a site visit, confirm the work order, verify that all required tools and personal protective equipment are accounted for, and review any site-specific access notes. Notify the dispatcher of your estimated time of arrival and confirm the contact on site.

Inspect the vehicle and load list against the job requirements. Any missing or damaged equipment must be reported and replaced before departure. Record starting mileage and fuel level in the daily log.

2. On-Site Procedures

On arrival, check in with the site contact and review the scope of work. Identify hazards and establish a safe working area before beginning. Follow all lockout/tagout requirements where applicable, and do not proceed if conditions are unsafe.

Document the pre-existing condition of the work area with photographs. Perform the work in accordance with the applicable specification, taking measurements and notes as you go. If the scope changes, obtain written authorization before continuing.

Upon completion, clean the work area, remove all debris, and confirm the result with the site contact. Capture closing photographs and obtain a signature acknowledging completion.

3. Safety Requirements

All technicians must complete safety certification before performing field work. Personal protective equipment is mandatory in all designated areas. Report any incident, near miss, or unsafe condition immediately, regardless of severity.

Maintain situational awareness at all times. Do not bypass safety devices or guards. When in doubt, stop work and consult a supervisor. Safety takes precedence over schedule in every circumstance, without exception.

Hazardous materials must be handled, stored, and disposed of in accordance with the relevant regulations and the site's material handling plan. Keep safety data sheets accessible for all materials carried.

4. Documentation

Accurate records are essential. Complete the field report before leaving the site, including time on site, work performed, materials used, and any follow-up required. Attach all photographs and measurements to the report.

Submit the completed report through the standard channel by end of day. Reports that are incomplete or submitted late may delay invoicing and follow-up scheduling. Retain a copy of all field notes until the job is closed.
