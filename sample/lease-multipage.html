Residential Lease Agreement

Lease no. RL-2026-0612 · Premises: 100 Maple Street, Unit 4

This Residential Lease Agreement (the “Lease”) is made between the landlord (“Landlord”) and the undersigned tenant (“Tenant”) for the lease of the premises identified above. The parties agree to the following terms and conditions, which together constitute the entire agreement between them.

Terms and Conditions

  1. Term. The Lease begins on the commencement date and continues for the fixed term stated in Schedule A, after which it shall continue month-to-month unless either party provides written notice of termination in accordance with these terms.
  2. Rent. Tenant shall pay the monthly rent stated in Schedule A in advance on the first day of each calendar month, without demand, deduction, or set-off, at the address designated by Landlord or by such other method as Landlord may reasonably direct.
  3. Security Deposit. Tenant shall pay a security deposit as stated in Schedule A, to be held by Landlord as security for the performance of Tenant's obligations. The deposit shall be returned, less lawful deductions, within the period required by applicable law after the Tenant vacates the premises.
  4. Use of Premises. The premises shall be used solely as a private residence for the persons named in this Lease and for no other purpose. Tenant shall not carry on any trade or business at the premises without the prior written consent of Landlord.
  5. Maintenance and Repairs. Tenant shall keep the premises clean and in good condition and shall promptly notify Landlord of any damage or needed repairs. Tenant shall be responsible for damage caused by the negligence or misuse of Tenant or Tenant's guests.
  6. Utilities. Except as otherwise stated in Schedule A, Tenant shall be responsible for arranging and paying for all utilities and services supplied to the premises, including electricity, gas, water, and waste collection, throughout the term.
  7. Alterations. Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions, or improvements to the premises without the prior written consent of Landlord. Any approved alterations shall become the property of Landlord upon termination unless agreed otherwise in writing.
  8. Pets. No pets or animals of any kind shall be kept on the premises without the prior written consent of Landlord, which consent may be subject to reasonable conditions and an additional deposit where permitted by law.
  9. Entry. Landlord may enter the premises at reasonable times and upon reasonable notice to inspect, make repairs, or show the premises to prospective tenants or purchasers, except in the case of an emergency where no notice is required.
  10. Assignment and Subletting. Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet the premises, in whole or in part, without the prior written consent of Landlord. Any purported assignment or subletting without such consent shall be void.
  11. Insurance. Tenant is encouraged to maintain renter's insurance covering Tenant's personal property and liability. Landlord's insurance does not cover Tenant's personal belongings, and Landlord shall not be liable for any loss thereof except as required by law.
  12. Default. If Tenant fails to pay rent when due or breaches any other term of this Lease, Landlord may, after giving any notice required by law, terminate this Lease and pursue all remedies available, including recovery of possession and damages.
  13. Quiet Enjoyment. Provided Tenant performs all obligations under this Lease, Tenant shall peaceably hold and enjoy the premises during the term without interruption by Landlord or any person lawfully claiming through Landlord.
  14. Notices. Any notice under this Lease shall be in writing and delivered personally or sent by recognized mail to the addresses stated in Schedule A, or to such other address as a party may designate by written notice to the other.
  15. Governing Law. This Lease shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the jurisdiction in which the premises are located, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.
  16. Entire Agreement. This Lease, together with its Schedules, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements, whether written or oral, relating to its subject matter.
