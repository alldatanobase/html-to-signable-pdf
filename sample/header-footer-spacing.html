Quarterly Policy Brief

This brief summarizes policy updates for the quarter. Notice the spacing: the header sits at the very top of the page with a deliberate gap before this text, and the page number is parked at the bottom of the page with a gap after the body — both controlled purely by CSS inside the header/footer elements.

1. Overview

The header and footer are written once in the source document and lifted into every page's margins automatically. Because they are positioned with ordinary CSS, you can place their content anywhere within the margin band: flush to the body, centered, or against the page edge. Try widening the top margin in the demo to see the gap below the header grow.

Adjusting the bottom margin behaves the same way for the footer: the band grows downward, and because the footer content is bottom-aligned it stays pinned near the page edge while the gap above it expands.

2. Spacing Recipes

To add room between the header and the body, either increase the top margin (taller band) or add padding/margin to the bottom of the header element. To move a footer away from the body, bottom-align it and let the band height do the work. These two levers — band height and in-band alignment — cover essentially every header/footer layout.

The same document styles that apply to the body also style the injected header and footer, so classes, ids, and inline styles all work as expected. Keep header/footer content within the margin band; anything taller than the band is clipped.
